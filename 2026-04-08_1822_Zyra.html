Findings

Phase 1
LOWDRAFT – WIN CONDITION
—

Zyra is correct; your job is zone denial on Enemy Thresh's hooks

Ally Zyra's plants punish landing positions from close-range engage supports like Enemy Thresh — if you pre-plant where Enemy Thresh is likely to W-dash or hook, he takes root damage on arrival. Ally Akali and Ally Amumu both need disengage assistance since they dive into chaotic situations. Zyra's ultimate (Stranglethorns) is perfect for this — a well-placed ult zone-denies Enemy Thresh's re-engage after Ally Akali dives.

Fix: No draft issue. The issue was positioning execution, not the pick.
MEDIUMDRAFT – THREAT RECOGNITION
—

Enemy Jinx + Enemy Thresh is a high-range hook-and-execute bot lane

Enemy Thresh hooks, Enemy Jinx W (Zap, a long-range slow) can catch Zyra from nearly 1,000 units. Zyra's defensive answer is to stay outside Thresh W-dash range and use plants to punish any hook attempt. Instead, two of your three deaths were Jinx + Thresh coordinating on you in the bot lane.

Fix: Against Thresh, plant your seeds toward him, not between you and your ADC. Force him to walk through your zone to hook, rather than standing forward of your seeds where his hook reaches you directly.

red team's bot lane/jungle

First blood kill on Enemy Thresh, immediate trade-back death

At 02:17, you killed Enemy Thresh with Ally Senna assisting in red team's bot jungle — an aggressive level 1 invade. Four seconds later (02:21), Enemy Jinx killed you with Enemy Thresh assisting. Blue team then traded Ally Senna at 02:29. The score was: Blue gets Thresh, Red gets Zyra + Senna. Net result: -1 for blue in kills but Enemy Thresh, the primary engage tool, died, which was arguably worth it.

Fix: Getting first blood on the enemy support is good. The problem is dying 4 seconds later — after killing Thresh you should have flash-retreated immediately. You lingered in enemy territory while Enemy Jinx still had her abilities up.
08:05HIGHEARLY – POSITIONING
bot lane

Second death to Enemy Jinx, same hook pattern

At 08:05, Enemy Jinx killed you again in bot lane with Enemy Thresh assisting — the exact same kill combination as 02:21. Enemy Thresh hooked, Enemy Jinx executed. Two deaths to the same two-person combo in 6 minutes means your positioning didn't adjust after the first death.

Fix: After dying to a Thresh hook + Jinx execute at 02:21, stand further from Enemy Thresh's maximum hook range (~1,125 units) for the rest of the laning phase. Plant seeds at that distance to poke rather than walking up to cast E directly. If you're not sure of his range, stay behind Ally Senna — Ally Senna is a ranged carry who should be further forward than you anyway.
