3 Things To Work On

1.
Converting a Lane Lead Into Map Pressure
You had a +473 gold lead over Yone at 15 minutes while he was 1/5. That lead compressed to +111 by 20 minutes. The habit to build: after hitting a CS benchmark (~120 CS at 15 min) with a gold lead, use the next 2-3 minutes to shove the mid wave and roam for a kill or a dragon priority call. Standing in mid lane farming while an opponent at 1/5 buys items in base is how leads become even games. One successful roam or tower plate converts paper gold advantages into real ones.
2.
Vision Score – 2 Control Wards for a 31-Minute Game
Mid-lane vision standard is ≥1.0 vision score per minute, or ~31 for this game. You finished at 20. You placed 2 control wards: at 13:28 (good timing, right after a dragon fight) and 23:01 (late mid game). Three deaths in this game – your own at 23:45, Shaco's at 22:58, and Shaco's at 27:21 – occurred in corridors where a control ward in the river would have given advance warning. Control wards cost 75g and mid-laners should buy one every 1–2 recalls. Adding that habit doesn't change this game's outcome; it changes the next three.
3.
Build Completion and Third-Item Timing
You ended the game with Shadowflame + Luden's Echo + Spellslinger's Shoes + Needlessly Large Rod in inventory. Shadowflame was the correct third item and it was completed at 24:24 – good. The Needlessly Large Rod sitting as a component for 7 minutes before game end represents a habit: after buying the first component of a major item, prioritize the gold to complete it on the next recall rather than spending on other components. An incomplete Rabadon's Deathcap deals less damage than a completed Shadowflame. Once Shadowflame is done, the goal is to complete Rabadon's immediately rather than mixing components.
