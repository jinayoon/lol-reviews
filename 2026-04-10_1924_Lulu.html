Phase 2 — Early Game (0–14 min)
Phase Context — Laning Phase
Your lane Ashe + Lulu vs Viktor + Rakan. Viktor is a scaling mage playing ADC – he has significantly less range and early damage than a real marksman. You win extended poke trades freely. The danger window is Rakan's level 2 dash combo; he can chain W → E → Q for an instant kill setup with Viktor following. Keep W off cooldown and pre-position to answer that engage before it lands on Ashe.
Mid Ahri vs Yone. Yone statistically favors this matchup (54% win rate vs Ahri). Ahri can't fight him in extended trades — she farms defensively and looks to roam through her R. Don't expect early kills from mid lane; expect Ahri to make plays elsewhere.
Jungle Shaco vs Dr. Mundo. Shaco dominates the early game — Mundo is a late-scaling jungler with weak early dueling. Shaco pathed top-side to secure all three Void Grubs (8:37–8:52), leaving bot without jungle coverage during that window. Mundo recognized this gap and timed both ganks (9:52 and 11:02) to hit bot lane the moment Shaco finished his Grubs objective. After Shaco secures a top-side objective, expect Mundo to move bot.
Top Kled vs Skarner. Skarner statistically counters Kled (56% win rate). Kled needs early aggression to stay relevant — if he falls behind, he becomes a liability in team fights. Don't assume top lane will produce a lead; plan around Kled being even or slightly behind.
Your laning goal: harass Viktor off CS with Lulu Q whenever he steps up, track Dr. Mundo on the minimap after every 60 seconds without a sighting, and drop a control ward in the river brush before returning to your pre-gank push depth after any death or recall.

3 Things to Work On
1. Recognizing the Repeat Gank Pattern
Deaths 1 (9:52) and 2 (11:02) followed an identical sequence: Enemy Dr. Mundo entered bot lane through the same river path, Enemy Viktor and Enemy Rakan joined, and you died. The 70-second interval between deaths means you returned to the same push depth without confirming Dr. Mundo's location first. The fix is a two-step routine every time you return to lane after a jungle gank: (1) check the minimap for the enemy jungler's icon before pushing past the river entry; (2) if you can't see them, drop a control ward in the river brush before committing to your pre-gank depth. One ward before returning eliminates 2 of your 3 deaths in this game.
2. Item Sequencing: Shurelya's Before Oblivion Orb
Buying Oblivion Orb against Enemy Dr. Mundo was correct – his R heals roughly 20% of his max HP, and Grievous Wounds applies on any hit including Lulu's Q poke. Both items belonged in this build. The mistake was the order. Buying Oblivion Orb at 17:16 first pushed Shurelya's Battlesong to 28:28 – with only 3 minutes left in the game. The Shurelya's active was completely unavailable for the 23:35 fight where your team lost Ashe, Ahri, and you in quick succession. That active would have given your team burst movement speed to either pursue the engage or close the gap back to Ashe when the dive landed. Corrected sequence: Ardent Censer (14:13 ✓) → Shurelya's (~20–22 min) → Oblivion Orb (~24–26 min). Dr. Mundo's healing is still punishable with a short delay; Shurelya's impact is near-zero when the game is already in its final two minutes.
3. Frontline Positioning in Teamfights
At 23:35, you died in the blue side upper jungle while Ally Ashe was still at the river – you were ahead of your carry when the fight began. As Lulu, every ability in your kit serves one purpose: keeping your carry alive through an engage. That purpose requires you to be in range when the fight starts. The habit: when walking as a group, position yourself at Ashe's back-left or back-right, between her and the most likely engagement angle. When you see Enemy Rakan or Enemy Dr. Mundo make an aggressive move forward, the first action is W on them (polymorph to cancel the engage), not movement away. If you're further ahead than your carry when a fight starts, you've already lost the positioning check.
