3 Things To Work On

1.
Pick a Different Champion
The community of people who have played against you has reached a consensus on what choosing this champion communicates about a person. That consensus is available for free on any League of Legends forum. Therapy is also available. Both are worth considering.
2.
Vision Control – 1 Control Ward the Entire Game
You placed 1 control ward this game, at 29:20, for the Baron. Your support's vision score was 112. Enemy Dr. Mundo's vision score was 43. Yours was 28 — below the enemy jungler you outclassed in every other metric. This is a skill issue. Three of your four deaths came from being caught in corridors that a single control ward would have covered. Buy 1–2 per recall, place them in high-traffic paths — mid river, baron-side entry, top tri-brush. 75 gold each. The 84-point gap between you and your support is not a support problem.
3.
Post-Objective Discipline
Three of your four deaths followed an identical pattern: secure something, stay, die. Death 1 at 13:25: secured 2 kills, died 1 second later to Viktor cleanup. Death 2 at 19:58: secured dragon, died 1 second later to all five enemies in the same pit. Death 3 at 22:58: over-extended in top jungle after a reset. The habit to build: after any objective or kill, before doing anything else, identify the enemies you cannot see on the minimap. If you can't account for them, use Deceive and leave immediately. The dragon at 19:57 was the correct objective. Everything that happened in the 1 second after it was taken was not.
4.
Visibility and Movement
Shaco's kit works when the enemy doesn't know where you are. The 2:57 first blood, all three Void Grubs, both Yone ganks — every positive moment in this game was built on the enemy reacting to you rather than anticipating you. Deaths 2, 3, and 4 inverted that: you were moving through corridors Viktor could predict, with no wards in place and Deceive either on cooldown or unused. The corrective: before entering any corridor where Viktor might be waiting, place a ward first or hold Deceive for a reactive flash rather than an escape. When the enemy jungler knows where you are, the kit stops working. The ward is what keeps them from knowing.
