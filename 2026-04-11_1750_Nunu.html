Phase 1: Draft
✓ LOWDRAFT – WIN_CONDITION

Nunu & Willump mid: roam-heavy tank with unconventional lane expectations

Nunu & Willump mid is an atypical pick that trades laning pressure for roam speed (W: Biggest Snowball Ever!), tankiness, and teamfight engage. Against Enemy Ahri – a slippery, mobile mage – Nunu & Willump cannot reliably trade in lane and wins through roaming to side lanes and diving the backline. Win condition: don't feed Ahri, roam to bot for Tristana, snowball into teamfights.

The comp justified this pick. The execution was partially correct – the roaming worked; the laning phase did not.
Phase 2: Early Game (0–14 min)
4:22 – 6:25dragon pit / mid laneHIGHEARLY – DECISION

Two deaths to Enemy Ahri before 7 minutes

Ally Nunu & Willump died to Enemy Ahri (+ Leona assist) at 4:22 in the bot side river and again at 6:25 in mid lane (+ Xin Zhao assist). Both deaths were against a mobile assassin in open terrain where Ahri's E (Charm) has free aim. Being 0/2 at 6:25 in mid lane gave Ahri enough gold to spike early and accelerated the CS gap.

Fix: Against Ahri, play behind minions to block Charm angles. In the bot side river at level 4, Nunu & Willump has no escape from a Charm-Ignite combo with a support assist. Avoid the river before level 6 unless you have Biggest Snowball ready and a clear escape path.
0 – 10 minmid laneMEDIUMEARLY – RESOURCES

CS deficit vs Ahri: -10 at 5min, -17 at 10min

CS at 5 min: 24 (Nunu & Willump) vs 34 (Ahri) – a 10-CS gap. At 10 min: 52 vs 69 – a 17-CS gap (≈250g deficit from farm alone). Ahri's Q (Orb of Deception) pushes waves quickly and she can clear safely from range. Nunu & Willump's Q (Consume) does not interact with wave clearing efficiently at low ranks.

Fix: When behind in a ranged vs. melee mid matchup, prioritize last-hitting under tower over fighting for CS in open lane. 52 CS at 10 min is approximately what Nunu & Willump averages in a losing lane – defensible, but the 4:22 death directly cost 2–3 minutes of CS that compounded the deficit.
Phase 3: Mid Game (14–25 min)
0 – 35 minentire mapHIGHMID – RESOURCES

Vision score: 5 in 35 minutes – effectively no vision contribution

Final vision score: 5. VS/min: 0.14. For context: Ally Lulu (support) was 3.10 VS/min; even Enemy Ezreal (ADC) managed 0.42 VS/min. Total wards placed: 1. Control wards purchased: 0. In a 35-minute game, Nunu & Willump placed approximately one ward and never purchased a control ward. This is not a role issue – mid laners are expected to ward the river brushes and enemy jungle entrances, particularly when roaming.

Fix: Buy a control ward every other back, minimum. Place a ward in the enemy mid-lane river brush before roaming bot. Vision score is not determined by role – it is determined by habit. A mid laner who roams frequently needs more wards, not fewer.

Phase 4: Late Game (25+ min)
24:46 – 25:26mid map / Baron areaMEDIUMLATE – TEAMFIGHT

Deaths at 24:46 and 25:26 during Baron fight

Ally Nunu & Willump died at 24:46 (Enemy Xin Zhao) and Ally Ornn died at 24:56 in the same fight. Then Enemy Xin Zhao secured Baron at 25:31. Blue team lost two tanks and the Baron in a 45-second window. Nunu & Willump's R (Absolute Zero) has a 2.5-second channel – if engaged before the channel completes, the ult deals no damage. This sequence suggests an over-extended engage attempt in the Baron pit without team follow-up.

Fix: Before using Absolute Zero near Baron pit, confirm your team is close enough to follow up within 2 seconds. The channel is a beacon for the enemy team to collapse on you. Use Biggest Snowball to knock enemies into your team, then R only when the fight has already started and you have damage-soaking backup.

3 Things to Work On

#1 Vision habits – ward on every roam

5 vision score in 35 minutes is the most correctable thing in this game. Nunu & Willump roams frequently through the river – that is the exact moment to drop a ward. Before every Biggest Snowball roam, place a ward at the river entrance. Buy one control ward per back starting at minute 10. This doesn't require extra time; it requires building the habit of buying one pink ward each trip to base.

#2 Surviving Ahri in early lane

Both deaths before 7 minutes were in open terrain where Ahri could land Charm freely. Against Ahri mid, use minions as Charm blockers by fighting directly behind the wave. In the bot side river at level 4 without a ward, Nunu & Willump has no escape from a full Ahri combo with support assistance – that roam was premature. Don't cross the river pre-6 without a ward confirming no enemies are collapsing.

#3 Baron fight engagement timing

The 24:46 death in the Baron area came from engaging without full team support. Nunu & Willump's value in the late game is Absolute Zero – a 2.5-second channel that requires your team to be within follow-up range. Engage with Biggest Snowball to knock enemies toward your team first, then position for R only after 2+ allies are in range. If your team isn't in range, don't initiate near Baron – disengage and regroup.
