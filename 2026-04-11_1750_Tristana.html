Phase 1: Draft
✓ LOWDRAFT – WIN_CONDITION

Tristana + Lulu: protect-the-carry scaling comp

Tristana + Lulu is one of the strongest late-game botlane duos in the game. Lulu's W (Whimsy) polymorph denies diver engages, E (Help, Pix!) provides a shield and bonus damage, and R (Wild Growth) anti-dive ult. Tristana's win condition: survive the lane phase against Ezreal + Leona, hit Yun Tal + Infinity Edge, then scale until the enemy team can no longer function.

Win condition executed correctly. You survived, hit the spike, and converted it into 15 kills.
Phase 2: Early Game (0–14 min)
6:43 – 10:07bot laneHIGHEARLY – DECISION

Two deaths to Enemy Leona: 6:43 and 10:07

Enemy Leona killed Ally Tristana at 6:43 and again at 10:07, both times in bot lane with Enemy Ezreal following up. Leona's engage pattern is telegraphed: she will E (Zenith Blade) → Q (Shield of Daybreak). Rocket Jump (W) cancels this entirely if timed within the 0.75-second lockdown window after E lands. Both deaths suggest Rocket Jump was either on cooldown or used offensively rather than held as an escape tool.

Fix: Against Leona + poke ADC, hold Rocket Jump defensively – it exists specifically to escape her chain. Use it offensively only when Leona is on cooldown or not in range. In the current lane state (Ezreal + Leona with poke advantage), using W to engage is the exact wrong trade.
0 – 10 minbot laneMEDIUMEARLY – RESOURCES

CS tracking vs Ezreal: even at 5min, -9 at 10min

CS at 5 min: Tristana 37 vs Ezreal 35 (+2). At 10 min: 67 vs 76 (–9). Ezreal's Q (Mystic Shot) allows him to last-hit minions at range without walking up, which lets him farm safely under his support's peel. The CS gap at 10 minutes reflects Tristana being pushed off the wave after the 6:43 death and conceding minions during the respawn window.

Fix: After a bot lane death, shove the wave quickly on respawn rather than playing to trade. Tristana's range advantage (690 base range at max stacks) means she can auto-attack minions from relative safety – use that range to catch up on CS before committing to another trade.

Phase 4: Late Game (25+ min)
0 – 35 minentire mapHIGHLATE – RESOURCES

0 control wards purchased in 35 minutes

Ally Tristana purchased 0 control wards across the entire 35-minute game. In a game that went to 35 minutes with Baron spawning at 20:00, ADCs are expected to contribute to vision around objectives on every back. Ally Lulu covered vision exceptionally (10 control wards), which masked this gap – but if Lulu had died early or been separated, Blue team would have had near-zero bot-side vision.

Fix: Buy 1 control ward per back from minute 15 onward. Place it in the dragon pit brush, Baron entrance, or enemy bot-side river. The habit costs 75g and removes one of the most common ways ADCs die in late game – walking into unseen enemies.

3 Things to Work On

#1 Rocket Jump discipline vs Leona

Both early deaths to Leona were in situations where Rocket Jump was either on cooldown or used offensively. Against Leona specifically, Rocket Jump is most valuable as a get-out-of-Zenith-Blade escape. The trade is: use W offensively to extend range and potentially kill Ezreal, or hold it as a Leona counter. When Leona is alive and in range, W should default to defensive use. Practice the timing: Leona E → brief lock-up → W fires. That window is about 0.75 seconds after Zenith Blade lands.

#2 Control ward purchasing

0 control wards in 35 minutes was covered by Lulu having 10. That's not a system – it's luck. In the late game, enemy vision near Baron and Dragon is what kills fed ADCs who walk into unseen angles. One control ward per back (75g) is the smallest possible investment for the highest-impact vision denial. Place it in dragon brush, Baron entrance, or river tribrush depending on the next objective timer.

#3 Post-death CS recovery

After the 6:43 death, CS fell from +2 to -9 by 10 minutes – roughly 11 CS lost to death timer and conservative play after respawn. On respawn after a bot lane death: shove the wave with auto-attacks from distance (Tristana's range scales high), don't commit to a trade while catching up. Getting 5–6 CS back before the next fight reduces item timing slippage.
