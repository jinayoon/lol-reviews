Findings

Phase 1: Draft
✓ LOWDRAFT – WIN_CONDITION

Lee Sin mid: roam-oriented fighter in an unusual position

Lee Sin mid is an aggressive pick that trades sustained lane pressure for early roam potential: Sonic Wave (Q) enables long-range gap close and kill setups, Dragon's Rage (R) can kick enemies into tower or team. Against Enemy Ahri – a high-mobility mage – the matchup is difficult in lane but favorable in skirmishes where Lee Sin can dodge Charm. Win condition: don't fall too far behind Ahri in CS, roam to bot for Tristana, find kills on Viego.

The win condition was partially executed – killed Viego at 6:04 and killed Ahri three times. The lane deficit and vision gap undermined the execution.
Phase 2: Early Game (0–14 min)
6:04mid map✓ LOWEARLY – DECISION

Kill on Viego at 6:04 – the most impactful early play

Ally Lee Sin killed Enemy Viego at 6:04 in mid map (with Shaco assist). Viego at 6 minutes had already killed Tristana twice (2:50, 5:30) and was building momentum. Stopping Viego at 6:04 – before his first item spike – was the highest-value kill of the early game for Blue team. This kill directly cost Viego a death timer and disrupted his first back timing.

Perfect target prioritization. Viego was the threat; killing him first was the right call.
0 – 10 minmid laneMEDIUMEARLY – RESOURCES

CS deficit vs Ahri: -15 at 10min

CS at 10 min: Lee Sin 68 vs Ahri 83 (–15). Last game on Nunu & Willump, the deficit at 10 min was also -17 against Ahri. The pattern is: losing the CS matchup in mid lane against Ahri specifically. Ahri's Q (Orb of Deception) hits both directions and pushes waves efficiently; Lee Sin's wave clear at low levels requires either Q-ward jump or burning Tiamat early.

Fix: Tiamat was purchased at 6:51 (unusually fast first item timing, before first back on most builds). If the Tiamat is available, use its active to clear waves between fights rather than fighting Ahri for individual last hits. The CS gap should be closer to -5 to -8 at 10 minutes, not -15.
Phase 3: Mid Game (14–25 min)
0 – 32 minentire mapHIGHMID – RESOURCES

Vision score: 18 in 32 minutes – second game with near-zero vision

Ally Lee Sin finished with VS 18 (0.56/min), 0 control wards purchased. Last game on Nunu & Willump: VS 5, 0 control wards. The champion changed; the vision contribution did not. Enemy Viego killed Lee Sin five times through mid-lane collapses – in every case, Viego approached from jungle or river. A single control ward in the mid-lane river brush would have provided 5–10 seconds of warning on Viego's approach, which is enough to Safeguard (W) to a nearby minion and exit.

Fix: Buy one control ward per back starting at minute 10 and place it in the mid-lane river entrance. This is not optional and is not role-dependent. Two consecutive games with 0 control wards purchased means this is a habit problem, not a game-state problem.
16:15 – 28:11mid lane / mid map / dragon pitHIGHMID – DECISION

Five deaths in 15-minute window: 16:15, 19:26, 24:25, 25:50, 28:11

Ally Lee Sin died five times between 16:15 and 28:11 – all five involved Enemy Viego (directly or assisting). The sequence at 16:14 is the clearest case: Lee Sin killed Ahri at 16:14, then died to Viego one second later at 16:15. After a kill, Lee Sin was still in the combat zone and Viego arrived immediately. This is the same pattern from Shaco's games: secure a kill, don't disengage, die to the follow-up.

Fix: After landing a kill on Ahri or any target in mid lane, immediately use Safeguard (W) to ward-jump to safety. Do not stay in the kill zone. Viego's dash range lets him reach you within 1–2 seconds of a kill – the 16:14 to 16:15 death was a 1-second window. Exit the fight the moment the kill registers.

3 Things to Work On

#1 Control wards – two games, same zero

Two games (Nunu & Willump last game, Lee Sin this game) with 0 control wards purchased and near-zero vision score. Viego killed you five times this game through mid-jungle collapses. Every one of those deaths had a ward-preventable window: if the mid-lane river brush had a control ward in it, you see Viego approaching 5–8 seconds before he reaches you. That's enough to W-jump to a ward and exit. Buy one control ward every back starting at minute 10. Place it mid-river. This is the single highest-leverage habit fix available right now.

#2 Disengaging after kills (the 1-second problem)

The 16:14 death (killed Ahri at 16:14, died at 16:15) is the clearest example of a pattern across three players this game: secure a kill, stay in the kill zone, die to Viego. Lee Sin has two disengagement tools: Safeguard (W) to jump to a nearby ward or minion, and Dragon's Rage (R) which can kick an enemy away and create space. Immediately after any kill in mid lane, use Safeguard to exit the area. Ward the jungle entrance before the fight so you have a Safeguard target available.

#3 CS recovery under Ahri pressure

Lost 15 CS to Ahri at 10 minutes for the second consecutive game against that matchup (last game was -17 on Nunu & Willump). Against Ahri's Q wave clear, fight for last hits only when you have a Safeguard target warded to escape Charm. Otherwise, freeze the wave near tower and let her push into you where her trading pattern is weaker. Tiamat was purchased unusually early (6:51) – make sure its active is being used to clear waves between roams rather than just as a stat stick.
