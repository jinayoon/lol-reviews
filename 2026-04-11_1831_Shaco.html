19:14 – 29:07mid lane / mid mapHIGHMID – DECISION

Three deaths to Enemy Viego: 19:14, 22:07, 29:07

Ally Shaco died to Enemy Viego at 19:14 (with Ahri + Brand + Rell assists), 22:07 (with Brand + Rell assists), and 29:07 (with Ahri + Brand + Rell assists). All three deaths shared a structure: Shaco was in mid-lane terrain when Viego, a champion that dashes through corpses and possesses enemies upon kill, arrived with support. Viego's passive (Sovereign's Domination) lets him jump into and possess a killed enemy – in a fight where Shaco's clone (Hallucinate) is also in play, the probability of chaos increases nonlinearly. Disengage with Q (Deceive) the moment Viego appears without your team in range.

3 Things to Work On

1. Pick a different champion
The community of people who have played against you has reached a consensus on what choosing this champion communicates about a person. That consensus is available for free on any League of Legends forum. Therapy is also available. Both are worth considering.

#1 Not engaging Viego without team backup

Three deaths to the same champion via the same mechanism – Shaco extended into mid lane, Viego arrived with support, Shaco died. Viego's kit specifically punishes isolated kills because he can chain-possess through corpses and reset his passive on takedowns. The correct Shaco response to a fed Viego on the map is: engage with team or don't engage. Deceive (Q) exists to disengage – use it to leave the fight when Viego appears, not to get into a better position within it.

#2 Completing the 5th item before the final push

Noonquiver incomplete for the second consecutive game. The pattern is: Shaco buys the component during the final push sequence when base rotation time is 8 seconds and there's no time to recall properly. The fix is planning the 5th item one back earlier. Before the push that takes the inhibitor, your inventory should have no components – buy the completed item on the preceding back, even if it means forgoing 200g of efficiency. Two Noonquivers in inventory is two lost items.

#3 Control wards: one per back, minimum

1 control ward in 32 minutes. Same as last game. Umbral Glaive's ward-clear passive operates on kills; control wards are placed proactively in key brushes before fights happen. A jungler with Umbral Glaive who also buys one control ward per back maintains near-total enemy vision denial around objectives. A jungler with Umbral Glaive and one control ward across 32 minutes is doing half the work.
