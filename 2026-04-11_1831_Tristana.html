Findings

Phase 1: Draft
✓ LOWDRAFT – WIN_CONDITION

Tristana + Nami vs Brand + Rell: a favorable lane matchup

Brand + Rell is a dive-and-burn bot lane – Rell engages, Brand follows with W → E → Q combo. Tristana + Nami counters this: Nami's Q (Aqua Prison) interrupts Rell's engage, Nami's W (Ebb and Flow) heals back poke, and Tristana's R (Buster Shot) knocks Brand out of his stacking combo. Win condition: survive early Rell dives, reach Yun Tal + IE, end the game before Viego snowballs further.

Lane matchup was favorable and was converted into a 917g gold lead by 15 minutes.
Phase 2: Early Game (0–14 min)
2:50 – 5:30mid mapHIGHEARLY – DECISION

Two early deaths to Enemy Viego roaming into bot: 2:50 and 5:30

Ally Tristana died at 2:50 to Enemy Viego (with Brand + Rell assists) and again at 5:30 (Viego + Brand + Rell). Both kills happened near the mid map / bot lane junction, suggesting Viego was rotating from mid to assist Rell's dive. At 2:50 Viego had already appeared in mid lane, killed Ahri at 3:18 (Shaco gank), and rotated down. No ward coverage on Viego's approach path meant both collapses arrived with no warning.

Fix: Ward the river brush between mid and bot lane when Viego is on the enemy team. A control ward at the bot-side river entrance detects Viego's rotation 5–10 seconds in advance. When Viego's position is unknown on the minimap, play under tower in bot lane rather than extending for CS in open lane.
✓ LOWEARLY – RESOURCES

Bot lane dominance despite Viego deaths: +917 gold at 15min

Despite dying twice in the first 6 minutes, Ally Tristana was +442 gold on Brand at 5 min, +715 at 10 min, and +917 at 15 min. Brand's 0/4 score by 15 minutes meant he was never a lane threat, and Tristana's CS recovered cleanly after each death (72 vs 64 at 10 min). Holding the gold lead through two Viego deaths without tilting off the lane is the key positive.

Good mental and lane recovery. Maintaining farm discipline after early deaths is what enabled the 19:45 IE spike.

0 – 32 minentire mapHIGHLATE – RESOURCES

0 control wards – second game in a row

Ally Tristana purchased 0 control wards in 32 minutes. Last game: 0 control wards in 35 minutes. Ally Nami carried the vision burden with 10 control wards this game (same as Lulu last game). In a game where Baron spawned at 20 minutes and Enemy Viego was the primary threat, an ADC with a control ward in the Baron brush or dragon pit provides vision that the support cannot cover while also peeling the carry. 3 Things to Work On

#1 Warding vs roaming junglers (especially Viego)

Three of six deaths this game were to Viego rotating into bot lane from mid. A control ward at the bot-side river entrance (between mid lane and bot lane) detects this rotation 5–8 seconds in advance. When Viego is missing from mid lane, play under tower until his position is confirmed. This is exactly the information a single 75g ward provides. Two games, 0 control wards, 5 deaths to roaming threats that a ward would have flagged.

#2 Rocket Jump as an escape vs engage supports

Two deaths to Rell this game (last game two deaths to Leona) – both dive-and-initiate supports that follow the same pattern: E → chain engage → Viego/Ezreal follow-up. Against Rell specifically, her Ferromancy: Crash Down (E) has a pull range of roughly 900 units. The moment Rell's E animation starts, Rocket Jump should fire toward safety. Practice recognizing the E cast animation as a Rocket Jump trigger.

#3 Control ward habit

Zero control wards in both games analyzed. This is the clearest correctable pattern across the dataset: you win lane, you scale, you carry – and you buy zero control wards while doing it. In both games the support (Lulu, Nami) carried the entire vision burden with 10 control wards each. ADC is not exempt from vision; the ADC is the player who most needs to know where the enemy jungler is. One per back. Dragon brush or Baron entrance. Non-negotiable.
