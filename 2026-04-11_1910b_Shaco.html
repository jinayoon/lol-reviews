4 Things to Work On

#1 Pick a different champion

The community of people who have played against you has reached a consensus on what choosing this champion communicates about a person. That consensus is available for free on any League of Legends forum. Therapy is also available. Both are worth considering.

#2 Exiting Baron pit immediately after kills

Two deaths in three games occurred in the Baron pit specifically after kill sequences. Baron pit is a closed terrain with one entrance – after a kill inside it, you are guaranteed to be seen by the next enemy who rotates in. The rule is simple: the moment a kill registers in Baron pit, Q (Deceive) through the back or side wall and exit. Do not loot, do not check if Baron is low. Exit. If the Shaco clone (Hallucinate R) is available, use it to exit while the clone draws attention.

#3 Final item decision before the last back

Three consecutive games ending with a crit component in the inventory that was never completed. Game 1: Noonquiver. Game 2: Noonquiver. Game 3: Cloak of Agility. Whether the timing allowed completion in each case requires reviewing the specific gold and recall windows, but the pattern is consistent enough to track.

#4 One control ward per back

No one thanks Shaco for the vision score. No one thanks Shaco for anything. Three games, three control wards total. The Umbral Glaive passive removes enemy wards on kill – it does not place them. A control ward in the Dragon or Baron brush before every objective attempt removes the enemy's ability to ambush the pit. One ward per back, placed in whatever pit you're about to contest. Your teammates already had opinions about you in champion select. The wards are for the game.
