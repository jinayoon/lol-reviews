Smolder + Lulu: scaling carry with scaling support

Smolder is a late-scaling marksman whose Q (Super Scorcher Breath) gains stacks and new effects over the game. Lulu is a natural pairing: Wild Growth anti-dive, Whimsy polymorph on divers, Ardent Censer buffing Smolder's attack speed. Win condition: farm safely, accumulate Q stacks, reach 2-item spike, then teamfight. Enemy Jinx is a mirror-role matchup that Smolder beats with mobility (E) and sustain.

Win condition executed correctly. 77 CS ahead at 15 minutes is the expected output against a Jinx who was 0/5 by then.

11:33LOWEARLY – RESOURCES

Essence Reaver first item: slightly delayed power spike for Smolder

Base

Ally Smolder completed Essence Reaver at 11:33 as a first item. Smolder's most common first items are Heartsteel (for Q stack scaling) or Infinity Edge (for immediate damage). Essence Reaver provides mana-on-crit and CDR, which is more valuable on champions that spam abilities frequently. Smolder's Q is the stacking ability, but the stacks from Q matter more later. The power spike from IE at the same timing would have been higher.

Fix: Minor – the game was already won in bot lane by 11 minutes. In future games: if you're ahead and the enemy team has armor (Shen, Pantheon), consider IE into Last Whisper rather than Essence Reaver. If the game might go long and you need mana sustain, Essence Reaver is defensible.
Phase 3: Mid Game (14–25 min)
17:50 – 20:26MEDIUMMID – DECISION

Deaths at 17:50 and 20:26 to Pantheon mid-game roams

Mid Map / Baron Pit

Ally Smolder died at 17:50 to Enemy Pantheon (with Jinx assist) and at 20:26 to Enemy Pantheon (with Ahri assist). Both deaths happened in the mid-to-top area where Pantheon was rotating from lane. Pantheon's W (Aegis Assault) makes him a mobile frontliner who can collapse quickly through jungle. No ward coverage on his approach path meant both kills arrived without warning.

Fix: Against a Pantheon who is roaming, place a ward at the mid-lane river entrance before grouping in mid. When Pantheon's position is unknown on the minimap, do not stand in open terrain. Flap, Flap, Flap (E) is Smolder's escape – save it when Pantheon is unseated rather than using it aggressively.

3 Things to Work On

#1 Control ward habit: three games, zero wards

Zero control wards across all three games is the clearest pattern in the entire three-game dataset for this player. It has not been punished yet (Blue team won all three) but the death at 20:26 near Baron pit is a preview of what happens when an ADC without vision meets an enemy roaming in unseen jungle. One control ward per back, placed at the objective you're rotating toward. The support cannot be the entire vision plan.

#2 Positioning after teamfights near Baron

The 20:26 death to Pantheon came immediately after Blue team's 3-kill fight at 20:00–20:09. After a fight near Baron pit, Smolder should immediately exit the pit (toward Blue team's base side) and recall or move to safety. Staying in Baron pit after a fight invites the surviving enemies to rotate back in for cleanup. Flap, Flap, Flap (E) should be used to exit rather than saved for the next engage.

#3 Item timing: plan the first item before the game starts

Essence Reaver at 11:33 provided mana sustain but delayed the damage spike vs IE or Heartsteel. For Smolder specifically: if the matchup is a winning lane (Jinx, any low-mobility ADC), Heartsteel first gives more Q stacks faster, and the passive scales throughout the game. If you expect the game to be close in teamfights, IE is the higher-damage first item. Essence Reaver is a reasonable third item but not optimal as a first purchase in a winning lane.
