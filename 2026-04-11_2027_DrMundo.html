3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Exit after kills in enemy territory – every time
Four documented deaths across this game following the same sequence: kill secured in enemy territory → stayed in area → caught by 2–3 enemies rotating. The timestamps are 7:11, 11:51, 29:15, 36:46. Dr. Mundo's kit (Heart Zapper W, Maximum Dosage R) makes him feel tanky enough to absorb a follow-up, but Nami+Aphelios and Azir+Aphelios+Nami are not fights that durability wins in the late game. The rule: after any kill in enemy half of the map, press B immediately. No exceptions for 'Baron is low' or 'they're close.' Exit.
2. Control wards as the jungler
1 control ward in 42 minutes as the jungler. The jungler is in the best position to place control wards in objective brushes because they pass through those areas on every rotation. Before every Dragon or Baron contest, buy a control ward on the preceding back and place it in the objective brush. This removes the enemy's ability to set up ambushes inside the pit. The cost is 75 gold. The value is objective safety. Over 5 games in this dataset, the jungler has averaged under 1 control ward per game.
3. Death timer awareness in late game
The 29:15 death came 49 seconds after Blue team secured Baron at 28:26. At 29 minutes, death timers are approximately 40 seconds. Dying on Baron buff wastes ~40 seconds of the buff's duration (it lasts 3 minutes 30 seconds). With Baron active, Dr. Mundo should be the last person in a fight to die – absorb damage at the front while Ezreal and Ahri output from range, then recall when the fight is decided. Don't commit to 1v3 fights while the team needs the buff carrier alive.
