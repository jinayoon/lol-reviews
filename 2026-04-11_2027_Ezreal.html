14:50 and 18:10 | bot laneHIGHMID – DECISION
Deaths at 14:50 and 18:10 – Zac+Nami ganking without ward coverage
Ezreal died at 14:50 to Aphelios+Zac+Nami and at 18:10 to Aphelios+Zac+Nami. Both deaths came from the same three-person combination collapsing from the same direction – Zac engages from river, Nami amplifies, Aphelios follow-up. No ward coverage on the Zac approach path either time. Zac's Elastic Slingshot (E) travels approximately 1,550 units – he can initiate from outside standard ward range if wards are placed at the brush entrance rather than deeper in the river.
Fix: Against a Zac+Nami combination, place the river ward at the deeper jungle brush (not just the river entrance) so Elastic Slingshot's approach is visible earlier. When Zac's position is unknown on the minimap, stand closer to Blue team's tower and use Mystic Shot (Q) to poke from range rather than walking up to last-hit. Arcane Shift (E) should be held for Zac's engage, not used aggressively.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Control wards: 75 gold, four games
0 control wards across Shaco (x3) and Ezreal (x1) confirms this is not a champion-specific behavior. It is a shopping habit. The direct consequence in this game: deaths at 14:50 and 18:10 were approach-angle ambushes from Zac that a control ward in the river brush would have revealed. 75 gold. One purchase on the back at 10 minutes. One ward placed in the river brush. The fact that this hasn't been corrected after four games means it needs to become a conscious checklist item, not something to remember mid-game.
2. Arcane Shift timing vs Zac engage
Arcane Shift (E) is Ezreal's escape tool. Its cooldown is 19–13 seconds, which means it's available frequently. The problem: if E is used aggressively (to reposition for a Q or to dodge a skillshot), it has a brief cooldown window when Zac's Elastic Slingshot lands. The rule against Zac: hold E specifically for his E engage. Don't use it offensively in lane when Zac is on the minimap and unaccounted for. After Zac engages and you E away, immediately press B if you're under 60% HP because Nami will be following up.
3. Complete an item before buying a new component
Four games with incomplete components at game end. The common structure: near the end of the game, a cheap component (Pickaxe, Noonquiver, Cloak of Agility) is purchased because it's the most gold-efficient purchase at that moment. But it never gets completed. The fix: don't buy a component unless you have the full item price available right now, or you intend to complete it on the very next back. If you only have 875g for Pickaxe when a 6th item costs 2,800g+, save for one more wave rather than buying the piece.
