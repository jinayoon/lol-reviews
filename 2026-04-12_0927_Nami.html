Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Blue comp identity: Protect-the-Vayne with poke and engage — Nami peel, Viktor disengage, Volibear/Darius frontline. Win condition: survive laning, scale Vayne to 3 items, teamfight.
Red comp identity: Kill-lane bot (Jhin + Thresh), snowball jungler (Hecarim), and two safe farmers (Malzahar, Illaoi). Win condition: win bot early, leverage Hecarim with free drake stacking.
Notable mismatch: Jhin + Thresh is a level 2–3 all-in lane that punishes any step past the river. Nami has no reliable escape (no dash), making passive laning mandatory when behind. This was not respected (see Phase 2).
Credit: No major draft error on Nami's side. Imperial Mandate is a correct mythic choice — Nami's E (Tidecaller's Blessing) tags each hit with the IM mark, triggering on ally empowered attacks.

The 3 Swing Moments

05:44 — First bot-lane death

A 0-kill Jhin with a free kill became a 1-kill Jhin with gold to power-spike earlier. Bot lane never recovered the gold gap, and Jhin finished 13/2/11.

06:29–18:38 — Hecarim takes 3 drakes completely uncontested

No blue-side ward was placed in the dragon pit before any of the three spawns. Four-drake soul (or 4-stack Dragon buff at this patch) and the accumulating gold from objective bounties made red's mid-game unstoppable.

18:01 — Third death to the same Jhin/Thresh combination

At this point blue was already losing decisively; Nami's death did not change the outcome, but it is the pattern that most directly answers "what can I control?" — and the answer is: do not extend into enemy territory when behind and when that exact kill-lane is still dominant.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Lane depth against kill-lanes

Nami has no escape tool. Against Jhin (4th-shot root) + Thresh (hook + E slow chain), even 1 step into the river is a potential death. Establish a mental "safe depth" at the river entrance and do not cross it unless:

  • Ally jungle is confirmed nearby on the minimap, OR
  • You have a level or item advantage confirmed.

Practice call: In your next game against any hook-engage support (Thresh, Blitzcrank, Nautilus, Leona), mark a position on the minimap at the start of lane and do not walk past it without jungle confirmation.

2. Support ward rotation around objectives

The dragon/Baron ward timing is a rotation you can run on a fixed clock:

  • 4:30 → ward dragon pit entrance (control ward preferred)
  • 7:00 → renew dragon pit ward before second spawn; Blast Cone or tri-bush ward if possible
  • ~14:00 → baron pit entrance as game transitions to mid-late

Nami's raw ward count (29 placed, 7 control) is excellent — the issue is where they went. Prioritize objective vision over pixel-by-pixel lane coverage when the jungler is snowballing.

3. Second item: Ardent Censer over Dream Maker with Vayne

Vayne's W (Silver Bolts) applies true damage every 3 on-hit stacks — attack speed directly accelerates this. Ardent Censer's in-combat attack speed buff (20–35% per application on heal/shield) makes each of Nami's heals and Riptide ticks a combat multiplier. Dream Maker delays this synergy by a full item slot. Build order to target:

  • Imperial Mandate → Ardent Censer → Ionian Boots of Lucidity → Moonstone Renewer
