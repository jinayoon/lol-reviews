Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Blue comp identity: MasterYi snowball comp enabled by Yasuo ult + Zyra CC. Win condition: Yi gets ahead in the jungle, chains kills through Alpha Strike resets while Zyra zones and roots targets in place.
Red comp identity: Rakan engage + dive composition (Garen top, Briar jungle) with a scaling backline (Viktor, Xayah). Win condition: Rakan Grand Entrance + Garen dive collapses onto the carry before MasterYi can respond.
Credit: Zyra is an excellent draft into this comp. Her plants apply Rylai's slow to keep enemies in Yi's Alpha Strike cleanup range, and E root disrupts Rakan's W engage momentum. Arcane Comet keystone synergizes with E root (comet fires while target is CC'd).

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Boot upgrade timing — Sorcerer's Shoes by back 3–4

Basic Boots were held from 7:04 to 23:48 across the entire game. Sorcerer's Shoes grant 18 magic penetration — that's the difference between champions appearing to have ~30% resistance and ~10% on a 40-MR target.

Target build path:

  • Back 1 (~7 min): Fated Ashes + basic Boots
  • Back 2 (~10–11 min): finish Liandry's components or grab Sorcerer's Shoes if gold allows
  • Back 3 (~13–15 min): complete Sorcerer's Shoes before moving to Rylai's

Practice call: In your next Zyra game, set a mental note at your second back — if you have basic Boots and 800+ leftover gold after your main purchase, convert them immediately.

2. Control ward cadence — one per back, no exceptions

4 control wards in 25 minutes across 6+ backs. The fix is mechanical, not tactical:

  • Every recall → open shop → buy a Control Ward (75g) before anything else
  • If you already have 2 Control Wards in inventory, skip; otherwise buy one

Why it matters: In this game you had dominant vision anyway and red never contested dragon. In a closer game, missing a Control Ward at dragon pit at 17:30 means Enemy Hecarim takes drake undetected. The habit pays dividends exactly when you most need it — in games where you're behind.

Raw target: ≥1 control ward per back = 6–8 control wards in a 25-minute game. You bought 4.

3. Post-objective positioning — trail the push, don't lead it

After blue took the bot inhibitor at 20:02, Zyra was caught by Briar at 20:50 while at the front of the subsequent push. As Zyra, your value in a winning push is: 1. E root on any diver before they reach the carry 2. Plant zones blocking enemy retreat paths 3. Liandry's burn melting structures from behind the front

None of these require being the first person through the inhib breach. Let Mordekaiser (R isolation) and MasterYi (Alpha Strike invulnerability) absorb the front-line risk. Stay 300–400 units behind them — still in range to E anything that jumps through.

Practice call: After every objective capture this week, consciously step behind your frontliner before following the push.
