Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Matchup — Nami vs. AurelionSol bot: AurelionSol is a roaming mage played bot. His Q (Breath of Light) is a sustained beam poke with decent range (~875 units). His primary early game advantage is range and damage output against aggressive supports who stand in his sightlines. Nami's kit rewards aggressive play (W heal procs on enemies, E gives allies bonus poke damage), but against a champion with this much poke range, positioning is everything — you must stay behind Sivir and bait his Q onto her (who can Spell Shield it) rather than eating it yourself.
Win condition as Nami: Nami's Imperial Mandate is the right item here — her Q (Aqua Prison), E (Tidecaller's Blessing), and R (Tidal Wave) all apply Mandate's mark, which amplifies damage on CC'd targets. With Shaco and Vex providing additional CC/dive, your CC chain can burst priority targets. The plan: land Q bubbles in teamfights to trigger Mandate, use W to sustain Sivir through poke, and save R for disengage or engage opportunities.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Positioning vs. AurelionSol's Q — the specific fix

AurelionSol's Q (Breath of Light) requires a sustained lock to deal full damage. Two ways to counter it:

1. Use terrain: Lane bushes break his Q lock — step into the side brush when he targets you, let the beam drop, then step out to CS/trade.

2. Use Sivir as the bait: Sivir's E (Spell Shield) blocks AurelionSol's Q and costs him mana. Stand behind Sivir when his Q is up — if he fires at her, she can shield; if he fires at you, you're already in position to retreat into brush.

The anti-pattern to break: Nami tends to stand in the lane opening (between the brushes) at the same depth as Sivir. This creates a larger AoE target — pull back 200+ units behind Sivir's position when you don't have an active Q bubble to throw.

2. After two deaths in lane — adjust position or rotate

A two-death rule for bot lane:

  • After death 1: Move your starting position 200–300 units further back from the enemy. You can still ward and support from this range.
  • After death 2 to the same enemy: You cannot safely lane against that matchup anymore. Play under your tier-1 turret or rotate to mid. Continuing to lane in a position that has already gotten you killed twice gives the enemy a guaranteed 3rd kill.

This rule would have prevented deaths 3 and 4 to AurelionSol in this game.

3. Late-game support positioning — stay within 2-teammate radius

From 20 minutes onward, Nami's survival requires proximity to teammates:

  • Rule: Never be more than 600 units from your nearest 2 teammates when out of base
  • Why: Veigar's cage + burst is an instant kill on an isolated support. With 2 teammates adjacent, one can absorb the cage while the other assists you
  • Vision placement: Use the Oracle Lens (already in your build) to clear brush from close range with a teammate nearby — don't solo-walk into unwarded areas to place a ward. Ping a teammate to escort you if the ward placement requires entering a dead zone
  • R usage: Save Tidal Wave for two situations: (1) disengaging from a losing fight — wave knockback prevents the chase, (2) setting up a 3+ person CC chain when blue is engaging. Do not use it proactively to harass; conserve it for the critical moment.
