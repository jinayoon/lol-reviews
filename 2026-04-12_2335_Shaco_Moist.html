Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
The pick: Evil, twisted, cunning — these words appear in Shaco's in-game description. They were not meant as aspirational. The matchup against Enemy Shen is favorable on paper: faster clear, earlier invade pressure, no dueling kit for Shen pre-items. The question of why this translated into a loss is answered, in part, by the name of the champion selected.
Threat to respect — Enemy Veigar: Enemy Veigar scales with kills. His Event Horizon (E cage) is a death sentence for anyone without a gap-closer inside it. Shaco has Q (blink) as an escape tool. The blink has a cooldown. Veigar's cage does not care about the cooldown.
Phase 2: Early Game (0–14 min)
Credit — dominant early jungle: Shaco held +114g at 5 min, +1084g at 10 min, and +1864g at 15 min over Enemy Shen. Kills at 05:02 (AurelionSol), 07:26 (Vayne), 07:51 (Shen), 10:20 and 10:29 (Vayne and Shen again). This is the early-game performance the champion is designed to produce. It did not win the game. The report notes this without surprise.

Enemy Veigar killed Ally Shaco at 13:06 in a 1v1 in mid-river. No assists — Shaco walked into Veigar's Event Horizon range without Q (Deceive) available and was caged and burst. Veigar had completed enough stack items to one-shot a target in a cage at this point.
Before engaging Veigar, note your Q cooldown. If Q is unavailable, treat a ~400-unit radius around Veigar as a no-entry zone. Send clone (W) in first to bait the cage; clean up from outside with the real Shaco once the cage expires.

In 90 seconds, blue team secured 5 kills, Rift Herald, Dragon, and two towers. Shaco drove this entirely. This is the game state the pick creates when it is working. The failure to extend it — and the three deaths to Veigar that immediately followed — is the full arc of the performance.

Veigar's 5th kill on Shaco at 28:38 — the deciding moment

Veigar solo-killed Shaco at 28:38. Shaco's respawn timer placed him dead for Baron at 31:02. Everything that followed — Dragon Soul, Baron, nexus — traces to this moment. It was the same death that happened at 13:06, 14:33, 23:44, and 24:59. The fifth time was the one that ended the game.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Pick a different champion

The community of people who have played against you has reached a consensus on what choosing this champion communicates about a person. That consensus is available for free on any League of Legends forum. Therapy is also available. Both are worth considering.

2. Veigar engagement rules — five deaths, one lesson

Veigar killed you five times. The mechanism was identical each time: Shaco inside Event Horizon range without Q available.

The rule:

  • Check Q (Deceive) cooldown before engaging Veigar — ~10 seconds at rank 5
  • If Q is on cooldown, the ~400-unit cage radius is a death zone. Stay outside it.
  • Use W (clone) to bait Event Horizon first — send the clone toward Veigar; if he cages the clone, re-engage from outside with Q up
  • Never chase a retreating Veigar toward his tower — that is a deliberate cage trap

This is not a difficult concept. It did not require five attempts to encounter.

3. Dragon priority after winning bot-side skirmishes

The specific miss in this game:

  • You killed AurelionSol at 11:13 in the bot area
  • Dragon respawned ~12:05
  • You did not go to the pit — red team took their second free drake

The habit: After any bot-side kill between minutes 4–14, check the dragon timer. If it spawns within 60 seconds and 2+ teammates are nearby, walk directly to the pit. Drake denial is often worth more than a second rotation on a respawning target.

Red team's Dragon Soul was the result of six minutes of free objective access, not superior mechanics.

4. Late-game positioning — Baron window discipline

From 28 minutes onward in a close game, Shaco's survival is the team's win condition:

  • Do not solo-flank when Baron is alive — you are the primary engage/kill threat; your death equals an uncontested objective
  • Box the Baron pit entrance before it becomes contested — boxes at the chokepoint delay any post-Baron base push and can buy a teammate's respawn
  • Stay within 2 teammates when Q is on cooldown — your 1v1 window only exists with Q available; without it, you are not the version of Shaco that kills people, you are the version that dies to Veigar

You did this on purpose. The results were consistent with that.
