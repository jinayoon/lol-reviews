Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Matchup — Sivir vs. AurelionSol: AurelionSol bot is an unusual pick — he is a scaling mage with strong poke (Q homing stars) but does not have the typical ADC burst pattern. Sivir's E (Spell Shield) is the key mechanic: blocking AurelionSol's Q costs him significant mana and negates his primary poke tool. Proactively shield-blocking Q makes this lane much more manageable.
Win condition: Sivir's teamfight ult (On The Hunt) is the blue team's engage-initiation amplifier — it gives the team chase speed to follow up on Shaco's or Nami's engage. The win condition is: survive lane, buy Essence Reaver, and be alive with ult available in teamfights.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Starting item sell discipline — Cull and Doran's items

Two-part rule:

  • Cull: Watch for the in-game notification that says 'Cull passive complete.' Sell it on your next base visit, no exceptions.
  • Other starting items (Doran's Blade, etc.): Sell them when your 5th item slot needs to be filled — they provide diminishing returns compared to completed items after 20 min.

At 32 minutes Sivir had 5 slots used (Essence Reaver, Navori, Infinity Edge, Last Whisper, Executioner's Calling) + Cull. Selling Cull (~180g back) and buying a Phantom Dancer, Blade of the Ruined King, or Guardian Angel would have been a direct stat improvement in the final fights.

2. Control ward habit as ADC

ADC is the highest-value kill target on your team. Being caught without vision is the most common cause of ADC deaths. Build this into your back routine: 1. Finish purchases 2. Buy 1–2 control wards (150g max) 3. On the walk back, place one in the bot lane pixel brush (pre-6), river entrance (mid game), or baron pit approach (late game)

Against a team with AurelionSol's roaming threat, a single denied ward in river directly prevents one death.

3. AurelionSol positioning — read his W before committing

AurelionSol's W (Astral Flight) is a flying dash where he deals damage in a large area below him. When he activates W:

  • The zone deals AoE damage while he's in flight
  • You have ~0.5–0.75 seconds to move outside the radius before he lands

In-fight rule: If you see AurelionSol float upward, immediately move perpendicular or backward. Don't continue attacking — the landing zone deals more damage than your DPS in the time it takes to clear range.

ADC positioning principle: Stay behind your frontline at all times. If Shen or AurelionSol is engaging, your frontline (Urgot) should absorb it — position 400+ units behind the point of initial engagement.
