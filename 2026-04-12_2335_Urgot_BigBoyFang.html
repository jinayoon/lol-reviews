Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Matchup context: Urgot into Vayne is a lane Urgot can win pre-6. Urgot's Q (490 range) outranges Vayne's auto in poke, and the W passive shred forces Vayne to either play short or take repeated chip. The game plan is to establish a gold lead by level 6, then join mid-game fights with a power advantage.
Win condition: Blue team's win condition is a mid-game snowball through Shaco/Vex burst + Sivir/Nami teamfight setup. Urgot's role is: win lane, join the mid-game 5v5 as a tanky frontline, and peel for Sivir. Splitting indefinitely after 20 min misses the comp's power window.

630g behind Vayne by 10 min — matchup being lost passively
Urgot held 2598g vs Vayne's 3228g at 10 minutes, a 630g deficit that grew from -318g at 5 min. In a lane where Urgot has kill pressure in the 1–5 level window, a deficit this size by 10 minutes indicates passive play — likely letting Vayne free-farm rather than contesting with Q poke.
At levels 1–3, use Q to poke Vayne off CS whenever she steps to last-hit. At level 3, if Vayne has burned E or is low, commit to the W-Q all-in; Urgot's E into his own body followed by shotgun Q is a reliable kill combo before Vayne has her first item.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Urgot early trade patterns vs. ranged/mobile tops

Urgot's kit is designed to punish passive opponents in short trades:

  • Level 1: E into short brush, Q poke whenever Vayne steps to farm
  • Level 3: W-Q combo into a Q re-proc is Urgot's all-in setup; if Vayne has no E or Flash, this is a kill at low-to-mid HP
  • Anti-pattern to break: Don't stand at max Q range and only last-hit. Urgot's Q is a trading tool — use it on 2-hit intervals whenever Vayne is in range, even if she isn't taking a full trade back.
2. Control ward habit: every back, every lane

Set a rule: before leaving fountain, buy at least one control ward. Three high-value placements for top lane:

  • Top river tri-bush (blocks Shen pre-R ward, prevents ambushes from river)
  • Drake pit (when walking to mid-game teamfights)
  • Baron pit entrance (from 20 minutes onward)

At 75g each, two control wards per back is 150g — roughly 2% of your total gold budget but eliminates the risk of dying to unwarded flanks repeatedly.

3. Item path decision-making vs. AP-heavy teams

When the enemy has 2+ AP carries:

  • 3rd item: Force of Nature (Negatron Cloak → complete it before buying armor components)
  • Rule: Never buy two different defensive components at the same time unless you can complete one this back
  • Sell Doran's Blade by the time you start your 4th full item; the slot is worth more than 450g in combat stats at that point in the game
  • Against Veigar's cage (E) and AurelionSol's W slow, Force of Nature's movement speed bonus on magic damage taken is specifically strong — it lets you exit cage range faster
