Findings by Phase

Phase 1: Draft
Matchup — Vex into Veigar: Vex vs. Veigar is a skill-expression matchup. Vex's Gloom passive (W) resets on dashes/jumps — Veigar's Q and R animation both trigger this. Vex's E (fear) can interrupt Veigar's R midcast and is a strong peel tool against his R-Q-W all-in. Veigar's Event Horizon (E cage) is the primary threat: getting caged means taking a full burst combo with no escape.
Win condition: Vex's win condition is burst: land R (Shadow Surge) to gap-close on an isolated target, follow up with Q-E-W for the full rotation. In teamfights, R reset off a kill allows a second engage. The comp's teamfight identity suits Vex — Sivir's ult gives chase range, Nami's bubble chains with Vex's fear.

3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Event Horizon dodge mechanics — Veigar's cage

Vex has no dash. Her only escape from Veigar's cage once it lands is Flash or waiting it out (taking full burst). To avoid the cage:

  • Read the cast animation — Event Horizon has a 0.75-second travel time and the circular walls appear 0.5 seconds after cast. Move perpendicular (sideways) to the center, not directly backward.
  • Keep Flash available for trading in this matchup specifically; it is your only escape if caged.
  • Zhonya's Hourglass: Buy it. Activating stasis in cage cancels Veigar's ongoing R, lets the walls expire, and eliminates the kill.
  • Pre-position: Don't fight Veigar in his optimal range (~900 units). Vex's R has 1200 range — engage from max R range so you can close, burst, and back out before cage lands.
2. Item decision tree — Luden's vs. Liandry's, and Zhonya's priority

The principle: your 2nd item should address either your primary threat source or amplify your win condition:

  • Against Veigar: Zhonya's Hourglass as 2nd item (3000g, Needlessly Large Rod + Cloth Armor path) — the stasis cancels his cage kill combo
  • Dark Seal rule: Only hold it if you can stay above 7 stacks consistently. If you die more than twice before reaching 10 stacks, sell it immediately for a real item component
  • Fated Ashes: Never buy if you have Luden's already complete — they are competing mythic paths
3. Late-game engagement target priority with Vex R

Vex's R (Shadow Surge) picks one target — picking the wrong one costs the teamfight:

  • Correct targets: Isolated ADC (AurelionSol), squishy support (Karma) — both die in one rotation and give R reset
  • Wrong target: Veigar (has Zhonya's stasis; cage punishes you if R misses or he activates hourglass), Shen (tank, won't die in one rotation)
  • Pattern to break: In the late game you were engaging frontline/Veigar first. Find the backline with R and let your team follow up on the cleanup.
