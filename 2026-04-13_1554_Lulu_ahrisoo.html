3 Things to Work On (Detailed)

1. Post-Kill Discipline — Disengage, Don't Chase

After securing a kill in lane, the default action should be to move toward your turret, not forward. The 02:47 double death happened because both Twitch and Lulu stayed near where Caitlyn died rather than retreating:

  • After a kill, Sona's Power Chord is often fully charged and ready.
  • Twitch is fragile at level 2 with no dash.
  • Lulu's E (shield) can buy Twitch a few seconds but doesn't save him from a Sona W silence + Power Chord combo if they're out of position.

Drill: After any kill in bot lane, count to 3 and move your champion toward turret before doing anything else. Let the wave reset your next play.

2. Sona Matchup — Stay Behind Minions, Shield Before Poke

Sona killed Lulu twice in the same lane zone using Q poke + Power Chord + W denial. Key adjustments:

  • Position behind minions: Sona's Q has a secondary hit that bounces to nearby allies — standing in open terrain maximises her poke value.
  • Pre-emptive E: Shield Twitch before he walks up to CS, not after he takes damage. This forces Sona to choose between poking Twitch (who's shielded) or Lulu.
  • Don't mirror Sona's aggression: Your damage output (4,494 vs Sona's 4,676 this game) is similar, but Sona has better sustain via W heals. The winning play is to deny her W value with an eventual Oblivion Orb, not to out-trade her directly.
3. Support Quest Item — Buy at First Back

Glowing Mote (the Dream Maker upgrade starter) was purchased at 08:25 — approximately 4 minutes later than optimal. At your 03:02 back you bought Boots + Faerie Charm (350g), but the Glowing Mote costs less and starts generating bonus gold on assists immediately.

  • Rule: Buy Glowing Mote at your first back, before or alongside boots. Even if you can only afford the Mote alone, prioritise it — every minute of delayed quest income is gold lost.
